Madras, a city worth visiting.

Madras, the city of my childhood, the city of my youth, the city of my dreams, was the best city in the world.

As someone who has enjoyed everything that Madras has to offer, I believe it is time to give back to the city. How does creating a webpage equal giving back to the city? I am not an architect. I cannot create a tower in my city's honor. I am not a poet. I cannot wax lyrical about my lovely city. I am limited in what I can do. So I decided to do what I know how to do. Use technology to showcase the greatness of this city.

Madras the multifaceted, the multicultural.

South Madras is famous for its posh localities. North Madras makes sure that Madras remains Madras by remaining untouched by time.

Madras is to the south, what Bombay was to a lot of people, what New York City was to the world arriving at its ports. A place where you went to make your living. A place that offered hope of a better life and more often than not, fulfilled your hopes and dreams. The city that took my grandfather in and gave him food and shelter, taught him his trade and saw him live his life surrounded by family and supported by the fruits of his hard work.

The beaches - oh the ever present beaches. Madras's beaches are as long as the city itself, providing merriment and livelihood alike to those that crowd its sands.

The temples - so many huge old temples that, it appears to the random observers that the city is built around these temples.

The food - kothu porotta, mutta dosai, kal dosai, ulundhu vadai, paruppu vada! Enough said
